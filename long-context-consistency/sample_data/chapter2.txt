Chapter 2: The Discovery

Elena spent her first week deep in the archives. The old manuscripts were fragile, their ink faded but still legible. She found references to the Valdari in texts dating back over a thousand years.

Thomas visited the library each morning, bringing fresh bread and news from the harbor. Thomas had become fond of the young scholar. Thomas's wife, Margaret, insisted on having Elena over for dinner every Sunday.

Thomas's daughter, Sarah, was curious about Elena's work. Sarah was 16 years old, with her father's height and her mother's dark curly hair. Sarah often sat quietly in the corner of the archive room, reading whatever Elena recommended.

One afternoon, Elena discovered a hidden chamber behind a false wall in the basement. The room contained a stone tablet covered in Valdari script. Father Aldric was astonished — he had worked in the library for decades and never known about this room.

"This changes everything," Elena whispered. Elena had green eyes wide with excitement as she examined the artifact.

The tablet described a Valdari outpost that had existed where Miravel now stood. According to the inscription, the Valdari had built a network of underground tunnels connecting their coastal settlements.

Marcus arrived unexpectedly from Thornfield. Marcus had heard rumors about Elena's discovery and wanted to see for himself. Marcus was 31 years old, tall like their father, with the same auburn hair as Elena but with sharp gray eyes.

"Be careful, sister," Marcus warned. "There are people who would kill for Valdari artifacts."

Elena noticed that Father Aldric had grown quiet since the discovery. The old priest seemed troubled, spending long hours alone in prayer. She wondered what he knew that he wasn't telling her.
