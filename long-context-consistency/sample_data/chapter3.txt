Chapter 3: Unraveling

The autumn storms made the sea impassable for weeks. Elena continued her research, now assisted by Sarah and occasionally by Marcus, who had decided to extend his stay in Miravel.

Elena had dark brown eyes that sparkled in the candlelight as she translated the tablet. Elena worked late into the night, filling notebook after notebook with translations and annotations.

Thomas mentioned that strangers had been asking about the library. Thomas was 48 years old now, but still strong enough to keep trouble away from the docks. Thomas had black hair that was neatly combed back. "I don't like it," Thomas said. "These aren't the usual traders."

Marcus lived in Ashworth, managing their family's trading business from the northern provinces. Marcus had sent word to his contacts to learn more about who might be interested in Valdari relics.

Father Aldric finally confided in Elena. Father Aldric had known about the hidden chamber all along. Twenty years ago, his predecessor had sealed it shut, believing the Valdari artifacts were dangerous. Father Aldric had striking blue eyes that fixed on Elena as he spoke. Father Aldric said he had kept the secret to protect the town.

"The Valdari were not merely scholars," Father Aldric explained. "They were practitioners of something the church would call heresy."

Sarah discovered a second entrance to the tunnels near the lighthouse on the western cliff. Sarah had been exploring the cliffs despite her father's warnings. The tunnels were extensive, stretching far beneath the town.

Elena realized the implications. If the Valdari tunnel network was real, it could reshape the understanding of pre-medieval coastal civilizations. But it also meant Miravel was sitting on top of an archaeological treasure — one that would attract unwanted attention.

Elena decided to write to the University for support, hoping that official backing would provide some protection. Meanwhile, Marcus arranged for private guards to watch the library entrance.
