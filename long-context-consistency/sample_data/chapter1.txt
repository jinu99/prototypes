Chapter 1: The Arrival

Elena had bright blue eyes and long auburn hair that fell past her shoulders. Elena was a young scholar from the northern city of Ashworth, where she had spent the last five years studying ancient texts. Elena was 23 years old when she first arrived at the coastal town of Miravel.

The town sat nestled between granite cliffs and the churning sea. Thomas, the harbormaster, greeted her at the docks. Thomas had gray hair and a thick beard. Thomas was a tall man with a weathered face. Thomas was 52 years old and had lived in Miravel his entire life.

"You must be the researcher," Thomas said, his deep voice carrying over the wind.

Elena's brother, Marcus, had arranged her visit months ago. Marcus lived in Thornfield, the capital, where he worked as a merchant. Marcus had connections throughout the coast.

Thomas led her through narrow cobblestone streets to the old library. The building was three stories tall, its stone walls covered in ivy. Inside, she met the librarian, Father Aldric. Father Aldric had kind brown eyes and thin white hair. Father Aldric was an elderly priest who had been the library's keeper for over thirty years.

"Your brother speaks highly of you," Father Aldric said warmly. "The archives are yours to explore."

Elena settled into a small room above the library. From her window, she could see the harbor and the lighthouse on the eastern cliff. She unpacked her notebooks and began planning her research into the lost civilization of the Valdari.
